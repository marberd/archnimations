# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Real-estate developers and builders commissioning architectural visualization. They are
pragmatic and outcome-driven, not design critics. When they land here they are deciding
whether this studio can produce renders that will help them pre-sell units, win approvals,
or close investors, and whether the studio is reliable enough to hand a real project to.
They skim, they want proof, and they want an obvious way to start a conversation.

Secondary: architects and firms who may sub-contract visualization or Revit documentation.

## Product Purpose

archnimations.com is the client-acquisition site for Archnimations, an active
architectural visualization, rendering, animation, and Revit consulting studio in Tudor
City, Manhattan. It exists to turn a developer or builder's visit into a project inquiry.
Success is measured in one event: someone reaches out. Every page should make the work
credible and make contact the obvious next move.

## Brand Personality

Refined, dimensional, and dependable. The studio has a real visual identity, an iridescent
violet-to-cyan mark, and that identity should be felt, but it is carried by craft, not by
effects. Voice is plainly confident: it states capability and shows built work rather than
performing cleverness. Think specialist studio with a recognizable signature, not agency
flash and not corporate template.

Three-word anchor: precise, luminous, grounded.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI landing page.** Gradient text, glassmorphism-by-default, rotating-word
  heroes, big-number stat strips, identical icon-heading-text card grids. The templated
  look that floods the internet. If a visitor could say "AI made this," it has failed.
- **Cold corporate template.** Stock photography, safe blue, soulless business-site
  layout. Trust does not come from looking like everyone else.
- **Cluttered and busy.** Too much on screen, no clear focus, the actual work competing
  with chrome for attention.

## Design Principles

1. **The work sells, not the chrome.** The renders are the argument. Layout and color
   frame and elevate the project imagery; they never compete with it. When in doubt,
   give the work more room and the interface less.
2. **Identity through craft, not effects.** The iridescent violet-cyan identity stays,
   because the logo committed to it. It earns its place through precise execution, real
   type, real color, real surfaces, not borrowed AI-era tricks like gradient text or
   decorative glass.
3. **Every screen drives toward an inquiry.** The success metric is contact. The path to
   reaching out is never buried; from any page, starting a conversation is the obvious,
   low-friction next step.
4. **Pragmatic confidence.** The audience builds things for a living. Speak plainly about
   capability, scope, and reliability. Show real projects with real locations and real
   detail. Proof over adjectives.
5. **Distinct, not loud.** Avoid both traps: the AI-generic lane and the cold-corporate
   lane. The site should look like a specific studio made deliberate choices, memorable
   without shouting.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Real contrast on every text surface (no light gray on tint). Full
keyboard navigation with visible focus states. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected
throughout, including the intro entrance and any scene motion. Interactive targets large
enough for touch. Alt text on every project image, written in the brand voice.
